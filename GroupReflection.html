Group

It is generally agreed upon by the majority of the group that the communication we committed ourselves to was of great benefit. To begin with we were all apprehensive about completing a group assignment with strangers from all corners of the country, as our individual differences and opinions could pave the way for potential disputes over how to attack the assessment. However, our open and honest communication helped avert such conflicts and we feel it was the foundation of our efforts.

A primary effect felt from the last assignment stemmed from our collaborative efforts on the website using GitHub. A number of the team felt we left it a bit late, and as such there a was a bit of a ‘last minute dash’ to complete the assignment to a high level. We remarked in assignment 2 that it was an area that could have been improved on by beginning work on the website earlier. This in turn also would have helped prevent clashes of numerous people working on the website at the same time. This problem was further exasperated by the fact that GitHub may take upwards of 10 minutes to commit any changes done by individuals. This problem was pre-emptively considered at the outset of assignment 3 to mitigate these issues entirely. We were very pleased with the results.

A surprising aspect that was universally felt was the way in which the team handled their individual tasks. Each of us put their hands up swiftly for work and were eager to help in contributing to the assessment as a whole. The breakdown of key components within the assessment, turning them into steps and sections allowed for easy allocation of time and resources to achieve our goals. It was well felt throughout the group, when each of our sections had realised and noticeable accomplishments.

Overall, we learnt how to work collaboratively and efficiently. It is our belief that this is the crux of the series of group assignments thought the course. Yes, we learned some topical components, but it is the two key components outlined in the outcomes we are most pleased to have learned.

CLO4: Identify principles of collaborative work and explain potential conflict resolution techniques

CLO5: Participate effectively and creatively in a team environment

It is these two outcomes we can take away with us as individuals, and apply to whatever field we choose to go in to. It is these two outcomes that bridge all industries, not just IT. We are extremely grateful to have learned them.
